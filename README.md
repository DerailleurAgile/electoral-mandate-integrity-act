# Electoral Mandate Integrity Act (Draft v2.1)

**A private member’s bill to strengthen democratic accountability in Canada.**

This repository contains the complete draft of the **Electoral Mandate Integrity Act**, legislation that would amend the *Parliament of Canada Act* and the *Canada Elections Act* to require a Member of Parliament who changes party affiliation to obtain a renewed mandate from their constituents through a by-election.

### Key Features

  - **Broad but precise definition** of “change of party affiliation” (including voting patterns, cabinet appointments, and public declarations).
  - **Comprehensive exemptions** for party dissolution, mergers, expulsion, ethical violations, and members sitting as independents.
  - **Procedural fairness**: Includes Speaker notice, 15-day submission periods, a 5-day "cure period," and a requirement for published reasons.
  - **Service Continuity**: Explicit protection of constituency services and office funding during by-election periods.
  - **Legal Robustness**: Explicit parliamentary privilege protection and a built-in framework for Charter s. 1 justification.

### Constitutional Compliance: The Oakes Test

The *Electoral Mandate Integrity Act* is specifically designed to satisfy the **Oakes Test**—the legal standard used by Canadian courts to determine if a limitation on a Charter right is "demonstrably justified in a free and democratic society."

To meet this standard, the Bill includes:

  * **Pressing and Substantial Objective**: Protecting the democratic right of electors to have their party-based vote choice respected.
  * **Rational Connection**: A direct link between the requirement for a by-election and the restoration of a democratic mandate.
  * **Minimal Impairment**: A 5-day cure period, broad exemptions for matters of conscience/ethics, and the ability for the member to immediately seek re-election.
  * **Proportionality**: Balancing the individual right of the MP to change parties against the collective right of the constituents to effective representation.

For more information on how Canadian legislation is evaluated under this framework, see the [Department of Justice guide on the Oakes Test](https://www.justice.gc.ca/eng/csj-sjc/rfc-dlc/ccrf-ccdl/check/art1.html).
